# Dayfare Support

Thanks for using Dayfare, a travel spending tracker for iOS.

## Contact

For bug reports, feature requests, or any other questions, email **cardpilled@gmail.com**. We aim to respond within a few business days.

When reporting a bug, please include:
- Your iOS version (Settings → General → About → iOS Version)
- Your iPhone model
- A short description of what happened and what you expected

## Frequently asked questions

### Where is my data stored?
All of your trips, expenses, photos, and notes are stored locally on your device. Dayfare has no servers and does not transmit your data anywhere. If you have iCloud Backup enabled, your data is included in your encrypted device backup managed by Apple.

### How do I back up or move my data to a new phone?
Open **Settings** inside Dayfare and use **Export Backup** to save a `.zip` file. On your new device, open the same backup file from the Files app and Dayfare will offer to restore it on next launch.

### Why does Dayfare ask for location access?
Location is used to record your travel route and to auto-detect your local currency when you cross borders. You can deny or revoke this permission in iOS Settings at any time without affecting other features.

### Why does Dayfare ask for camera, photo, or microphone access?
Only when you choose to attach a photo or video to an expense. Permissions are not used in the background.

### How do I delete all my data?
Delete the Dayfare app from your device. iOS removes the app's local container, including all trips, expenses, and media.

### Is Dayfare free?
Yes. There are no subscriptions, no ads, and no in-app purchases.
